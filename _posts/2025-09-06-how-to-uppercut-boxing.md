The uppercut is one of boxing’s most powerful and deceptive punches. Unlike straight punches, it travels from the bottom up, slipping past your opponent’s guard and landing at an unexpected angle. When executed correctly, it can change the pace of a fight in an instant.

{% include preview.html %}

## Why the Uppercut Works

The uppercut’s strength lies in its surprise factor. Because it comes from below, opponents often fail to see it coming, making it highly effective at close range. Combined with proper timing and precision, it can open up your opponent’s defense and set up devastating combinations.

## How to Execute the Perfect Uppercut

### **Start in Guard**
Begin from your guard position, keeping your hands up and elbows tucked in for protection.

### **Punch Upward**
Drive your punch upward, following a vertical path toward your target. 

<div style='text-align: center'><img src='/assets/blog/uppercut/uppercut_step_2.jpg' style='width: 300px;margin: 10px 0px 30px 0px; border-radius: 10px; border: 1px solid #ddd;' alt='How to throw an uppercut - boxing 101'/></div>

Rotate your palm so that, at the end of the punch, it faces you.

<div style='text-align: center'><img src='/assets/blog/uppercut/uppercut_step_3.jpg' style='width: 300px;margin: 10px 0px 30px 0px; border-radius: 10px; border: 1px solid #ddd;' alt='How to throw an uppercut - boxing 101'/></div>

###  **Use Your Hips and Legs**

Power does not come from your arms alone. Rotate your hips and pivot your lead foot to generate force from your legs and core. This lower-body engagement is what makes the uppercut explosive.

<div style='text-align: center'><img src='/assets/blog/uppercut/uppercut_step_4.jpg' style='width: 300px;margin: 10px 0px 30px 0px; border-radius: 10px; border: 1px solid #ddd;' alt='How to throw an uppercut - boxing 101'/></div>

###  **Stay Protected**
Slightly raise your shoulders to shield your chin and keep your other hand in guard at all times. Keep your elbow close to your body for maximum defense.

<div style='text-align: center'><img src='/assets/blog/uppercut/uppercut_step_5.jpg' style='width: 300px;margin: 10px 0px 30px 0px; border-radius: 10px; border: 1px solid #ddd;' alt='How to throw an uppercut - boxing 101'/></div>

###  **Return to Guard**
After delivering the punch, snap your hand back quickly into your guard position. Recovery is just as important as execution to avoid counterattacks.

## Key Points to Remember

- Punch upward from your guard
- Rotate your fist so your palm faces you
- Generate power through hip and foot rotation
- Always return to guard immediately

### Final Thoughts

The uppercut requires more precision and timing than most punches, but mastering it can give you a significant edge in the ring. By combining proper technique, lower-body power, and solid defense, you can turn this tricky punch into one of your most effective weapons.
