Boxing has a rich and specific vocabulary. Some terms come from training, others from fight night commentary, and many are used daily in gyms around the world. This guide explains **100 essential boxing terms** in clear language, useful whether you train, watch fights, or are just getting started.

### Boxing Stances and Guards

1. **Orthodox**: A stance with the left foot and left hand forward.
2. **Southpaw**: A stance with the right foot and right hand forward.
3. **Switch-hitter**: A boxer who can fight comfortably in both stances.
4. **High guard**: Defensive position with hands held high to protect the head.
5. **Peek-a-boo**: A compact guard with gloves tight to the cheeks.
6. **Long guard**: Using the lead hand extended to control distance.
7. **Cross guard**: Arms crossed in front of the face for defense.

### Core Punches

1. **Jab**: A fast straight punch thrown with the lead hand.
2. **Cross**: A powerful straight punch thrown with the rear hand.
3. **Lead hook**: A horizontal punch with the lead hand.
4. **Rear hook**: A horizontal punch with the rear hand.
5. **Lead uppercut**: An upward punch with the lead hand.
6. **Rear uppercut**: An upward punch with the rear hand.
7. **Overhand**: A looping punch thrown over an opponent’s guard.

### Punch Variations

1. **Double jab**: Two jabs thrown in quick succession.
2. **Triple jab**: Three jabs thrown in a row to control distance.
3. **Power jab**: A jab thrown with added weight and intent.
4. **Step jab**: A jab thrown while stepping forward.
5. **Pawing jab**: A light jab used to measure range or disrupt rhythm.
6. **Check hook**: A hook thrown while pivoting away from an opponent.
7. **Shovel hook**: A punch that combines a hook and an uppercut motion.
8. **Haymaker**: A wide, powerful punch thrown with full force.

### Body Attacks

1. **Body shot**: Any punch aimed at the torso.
2. **Liver shot**: A punch targeting the right side of the body under the ribs.
3. **Solar plexus shot**: A punch aimed at the center of the upper abdomen.

### Defense and Head Movement

1. **Slip**: Moving the head slightly to avoid a punch.
2. **Duck**: Dropping the body lower to evade punches.
3. **Bob and weave**: Moving under punches in a U-shaped motion.
4. **Parry**: Deflecting a punch with the hand.
5. **Block**: Using gloves or arms to absorb a punch.
6. **Cover up**: Tight defensive posture protecting head and body.
7. **Pull counter**: Leaning back to avoid a punch, then countering.
8. **Shoulder roll**: Defensive technique using the shoulder to deflect punches.
9. **Clinch**: Holding an opponent to stop exchanges.

### Footwork and Ring Movement

1. **Pivot**: Turning on one foot to change angle.
2. **Shuffle**: Short, quick steps to maintain balance.
3. **Circling**: Moving around an opponent laterally.
4. **In and out**: Stepping into range to attack, then stepping back out.
5. **Cutting off the ring**: Limiting an opponent’s movement by controlling space.

### Offensive Concepts

1. **Combination**: A sequence of punches thrown together.
2. **Feint**: A fake movement meant to draw a reaction.
3. **Counterpunch**: A punch thrown immediately after avoiding an attack.
4. **Timing**: Striking at the optimal moment.
5. **Rhythm**: The pattern and tempo of movement and punches.
6. **Fight IQ**: A boxer’s tactical intelligence and decision-making.
7. **Pressure fighting**: Constantly moving forward to overwhelm an opponent.
8. **Out-boxing**: Winning with movement, range, and clean shots.
9. **Infighting**: Fighting at very close range.

### Fight Outcomes and Scoring

1. **Knockdown**: When a boxer is sent to the canvas by a punch.
2. **Knockout (KO)**: A boxer cannot beat the referee’s count.
3. **Technical knockout (TKO)**: The referee or doctor stops the fight.
4. **Decision**: A fight decided by judges’ scorecards.
5. **Unanimous decision**: All judges score the fight for the same boxer.
6. **Split decision**: Judges disagree on the winner.
7. **Draw**: No winner based on the judges’ scores.

### The Ring and Equipment

1. **Ring**: The elevated platform where boxing matches take place.
2. **Canvas**: The surface of the ring floor.
3. **Ropes**: Boundaries surrounding the ring.
4. **Boxing gloves**: Padded gloves worn during training and competition.
5. **Hand wraps**: Cloth wraps worn under gloves for support.
6. **Mouthguard**: Protective gear for teeth and jaw.
7. **Cup**: Protective equipment for the groin.

### Officials and Roles

1. **Referee**: The official controlling the fight inside the ring.
2. **Judge**: Official who scores the fight.
3. **Timekeeper**: Person responsible for round timing.
4. **Cornerman**: Trainer or assistant in the boxer’s corner.
5. **Cutman**: Specialist who treats cuts and swelling.
6. **Promoter**: Organizer responsible for staging the event.

### Competition Structure

1. **Bout**: A boxing match.
2. **Round**: A timed segment of a fight.
3. **Championship rounds**: The final rounds in a title fight.
4. **Undercard**: Fights before the main event.
5. **Weight class**: Category based on a boxer’s weight.

### Boxing Styles

1. **Boxer-puncher**: A balanced fighter with speed and power.
2. **Swarmer**: A fighter who applies constant pressure.
3. **Counter-puncher**: A boxer who relies on reacting to opponents.
4. **Volume puncher**: A boxer who throws a high number of punches.
5. **Power puncher**: A boxer known for knockout ability.

### Training Terminology

1. **Shadow boxing**: Practicing punches and movement without a target.
2. **Sparring**: Controlled practice fighting.
3. **Roadwork**: Running or endurance training.
4. **Bag work**: Training using a heavy bag.
5. **Pad work**: Training with focus mitts.
6. **Footwork drills**: Exercises designed to improve movement.
7. **Recovery day**: A lighter training or rest day.

### Rules and Fouls

1. **Low blow**: An illegal punch below the belt.
2. **Rabbit punch**: An illegal punch to the back of the head.
3. **Holding**: Grabbing an opponent excessively.
4. **Break**: Referee command to stop clinching.
5. **Disqualification**: Losing a fight due to rule violations.

### Common Boxing Expressions

1. **In the pocket**: Standing in close punching range.
2. **Make him miss**: Evading punches through defense or movement.
3. **Make him pay**: Countering after an opponent misses.
4. **Pressure**: Forcing the opponent backward consistently.
5. **Feeling the distance**: Understanding and controlling range.
6. **Standing count**: Referee count given when a boxer is hurt but standing.
7. **Throw in the towel**: The corner signals surrender.
8. **Ring generalship**: Controlling the pace and positioning of a fight.
9. **Punch output**: The number of punches thrown.
10. **Clean shot**: A punch that lands clearly and effectively.
